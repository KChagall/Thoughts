About

Facing a crucial exam and afraid to fail? Going to a social event and hesitant to meet certain people? Someone treated you with disrespect in a way you can't stop thinking about it? A close person has entered a risky situation, and you don't stop worrying about his well-being?

Do these situations cause strong negative feelings and paralyze you? You are probably experiencing anxiety. Anxiety is an exaggerated feeling of uneasiness and worry. Anxiety disorders differ from normative anxiety by being excessive or persisting during long periods.

Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) is effective for anxiety. CBT focuses on challenging and replacing unhelpful thoughts, beliefs, attitudes, and behaviors. Thought records are a tool used in CBT to help you recognize your unhelpful thoughts, change them into rational ones, and reduce anxiety.

You can use the Thought records worksheet above anywhere and anytime you are anxious. Use it on your smartphone, computer, or print it if you want. Our goal is to make CBT therapy tools easy to use and available!
